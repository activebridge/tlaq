Tlaquepaque Arts and Shopping Village has provided the perfect setting for south of the border fiestas, festivals, and cultural extravaganzas for the last four decades. Unfortunately, this year we are dealing with the unprecedented COVID pandemic and must pause our highly attended Day of the Dead celebration. There's good news though! Our annual Marigold Mural project is back this year with a NEW SAFE AND EXCITING FORMAT.

Join us on Wednesday, November 4 through Saturday, November 7, from 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. in Calle Independencia. This project is unique to Tlaquepaque and an artful way to remember your loved ones. The artist Lovejoy will be in attendance again this year, handing out markers as she guides and answers questions regarding the mural and your contributions. Face masks and sanitizer will also be available for those who need them.

As in years past, Lovejoy will prepare the mural with her artistic interpretation of a traditional Day of the Dead motif. This year you are invited to bring or share with us a tribute to a lost loved one to add to our "Tree of Life, Messages of Love" themed remembrance wall. Suggested contributions include a letter, picture, or drawing that is 4" x 6" or smaller. Those tributes provided to us will be printed out and added to the wall by our team — please upload them at visitorinfo@tlaq.com.

Murals from previous years will be brought out of storage and placed in the main Calle of Tlaquepaque South to inspire you, or for those of you who have participated in this event before to find and revisit your remembrances from the past.

While you're here visiting and participating in the Marigold Mural Project, be sure to spend the day discovering Tlaquepaque and our inspired Day of the Dead vignettes and decor set up throughout the North and South areas of our village. Although we are forgoing many of our annual Tlaquepaque celebrations this year to ensure the health and safety of our visitors and community, you can enjoy our Holiday Tree Lighting online this year on Friday, December 4 at 5:30 p.m.
