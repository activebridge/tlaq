Your insider's guide to what's happening this September at Tlaquepaque!

## First Friday in the Galleries — Friday, September 6th

Visit our galleries on the First Friday of every month. Enjoy artists, receptions, music, wine tastings and treats, as you immerse yourself in the work of renowned artists from Sedona and around the World.

**Mountain Trails Gallery** presents "The Process of Creation: Wildlife and Beyond" featuring artists who work with intricate techniques in sculpture, painting, drawing, ceramics, and mixed media including wildlife sculptors Mark Edward Adams, Bryce Pettit and Raymond Gibby. Explore Sarah Siltala's layered aviary still life paintings, or the intricate desert creatures of Jennifer O'Cualain's wildlife world, or discover colored pencil wildlife and equine paintings by Sandra Byland, and more. Suite A-201.

**AZADI Fine Rugs**, Suite B219 — Sacred Symbols: Rugs are one of the original forms of art. Weavers were inspired by the world around them using sacred symbols and images of nature in their designs. Visit AZADI Fine Rugs to see a variety of examples of this woven art.

**Honshin Fine Art Gallery**, Suite C-205 — In September take the journey up 18 steps to the Gallery of the Ascending Spirit. Enjoy "Stone Soul Essence and the Language of Light" — new jewelry for men and women highlighting the symbolic use of light and color in the masterful artwork of 5 local jewelers.

**Rowe Fine Art Gallery** presents "Fruits of Labor", Suite A-102-3 — Come see what your favorite gallery artists have been creating all summer during this Labor Day celebration. Everyone will be talking about new works by oil painter Lynn Heil and sculptor Ken Rowe.

**Quilts Ltd Gallery, Tlaquepaque North** welcomes Rebecca Speakes, Suite A-14 — Southwest fiber artist Rebecca Speakes is presenting her latest hand quilted fiber works of art. Made of chintz, lame and batiks which are embellished with sparkly foil stamping.

**Andrea Smith Gallery** presents Sara Wiseman Book Signing & Discussions, Suite D-102 — Sara Wiseman, gifted intuitive, visionary teacher and author, had a near-death experience in 2000 which resulted in a sudden opening of consciousness. Meet and talk with Sara at the Andrea Smith Gallery, October 4-5.

## What's New at Tlaquepaque

**Vino Zona Sedona** — Enjoy a distinct and delicious experience. Founded by Ginger Mackenzie, Vino Zona first opened its doors in Jerome, AZ in 2018, to offer a curated Arizona wine tasting experience in an inviting environment. Ginger and her team handpick the very best wines that Arizona has to offer, including those that are exclusive just to her shops from small batch, out-of-the-way, local wineries. Whatever your taste in wine, Vino Zona has something for you, including a curated tasting menu of the best award-winning dry reds, whites and sweet wines all from the Grand Canyon State. Suite A207 | Open 10 - 6 p.m. Sunday through Thursday, 10 - 7 p.m. Friday and Saturday | (928) 282-2650

## Septoberfest at Oak Creek Brewery & Grill

Why wait until October? Oak Creek Brewery & Grill is known for its award-winning premium beers, brewed on site and stored in highly polished custom copper serving tanks behind its classic oak bar. Enjoy fabulous craft beers, big cozy booths, great food and fantastic views from this family-friendly restaurant.

September 10th through October 31st — stop by the Oak Creek Brewery every day until 5 p.m. and enjoy Drink Specials as well as $5.00 pretzels, $13.00 Pork Schnitzel Sandwich and $13.00 Currywurst sandwich all day long! Special seasonal beer "Marzen" is the featured beer while supplies last. Oak Creek Brewery & Grill Suite D101, open daily at 11:30 a.m. | 928-282-3300

## Sedona Flamenco Release Party — Sunday, September 8th at 5:30 p.m.

Join master guitarist Gaetano for spirited musical performances. Presented by Tlaquepaque's El Rincon Restaurante Mexicano, enjoy your favorite beverage from the restaurant's outside bar. Sunday through Thursday evenings until October 29th from 5:30 - 7:30 p.m. El Rincon Suite A112 | 928-282-4648

## Family Friendly Game Night — Friday, September 13th, 5 - 7 p.m.

Tlaquepaque Toy Town is hosting their first Family Friendly Game Night. Free for all. Stop by and prepare to laugh and have some fun. There will be prizes, a cash bar hosted by El Rincon and some light snacks. Any questions? Call Tlaquepaque Toy Town at 928-282-1087.

## Red Earth Theater Presents "A Midsummer Night's Dream"

Red Earth Theater presents live performances each third Thursday of each month. The fall season will kick off with Midsummer Night's Dream, performed in collaboration with the Flagstaff Shakespeare Festival September 15 - September 19th. Check out RedEarthTheatre.org for tickets and more information.
