## Gatsby-Themed Gala to Be Sedona Soiree of the Season

A spectacular night of jazzy reveling and 1920s elegance will indulge Sedona's desire to experience one of the most enticing eras of the century! The Swingin' Soiree will kick off at Tlaquepaque on Friday, May 11th at 7:30 p.m. Attendees are encouraged to wear 1920s attire, black-tie optional dress for the glamorous Gatsby event.

Entry includes live entertainment, dancing, four complimentary cocktails, passed hors d'oeuvres throughout the night and a $50 gaming voucher. The amazing restaurant Rene at Tlaquepaque will offer a separate 3 course prix-fixe meal before the event begins "Gatsby Style" for $45.00 plus gratuity and tax. Appetizers are a choice of Oysters Rockefeller or Waldorf Salad. Entrée is a choice of Tournedos Rossini or Sea Bass en Papillote and the dessert is a choice of chocolate mousse or coupe maison. Please call Rene at 282-3225 directly to make your reservation.

Guests are invited to test their luck at Craps and Roulette gaming tables. Additional $50 vouchers can be purchased for $20 each. Raffle prizes will be offered for our gambling winners including a wine pairing dinner for up to six people from the Pumphouse Station by Chef David Schmidt. Small bites will be provided by Chef Ron Moler with Something Elaborate Catering. Be sure to save room for dessert! Chocolate covered strawberries and an assortment of sweets provided by local favorite Sedona Cake Couture will have your sweet tooth covered. After you've indulged, stop by our whiskey bar and try something extra special.

The highlight of the evening will be the entertainment and special show featuring swanky dancers from the Swingtips. Anyone who has ever seen the Swingtips perform knows how much fun it is to be a part of the band's live, interactive shows. Since 1996, audiences of all ages have found themselves laughing, singing, dancing, and even performing on stage with the band in the tradition of Vaudeville's golden age of variety entertainment with a modern twist.

The Swingtips can be traced back to Kregg Barentine, who in 1992 was responsible in part for the Zony-award winning 1930-40's swing revue, *The All-Night Strut*, which played to sold-out crowds each night. After touring with The Glenn Miller Orchestra, Barentine returned to the Phoenix-metro area and continued to perform with the Dorsey Orchestra, Wayne Newton, Temptations, Bobby Vinton, Bob Newhart, Tito Puente Jr., and others.

Other special surprises will await the guests at Tlaquepaque including free tarot card readings and a special surprise in our fountain. Last but not least, Renee Taylor Gallery has kindly donated a $5,000.00 Simon G diamond necklace to be raffled off during the evening. Raffle tickets will be $20.00 each or $100.00 for six.

Tlaquepaque is sponsoring the event and all proceeds will benefit the Sedona International Film Festival! Space is limited to 150 people for this very special event on Friday, May 11th.

*"A Little Party Never Killed Nobody"*
